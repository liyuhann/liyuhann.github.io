Research

My research on the mechanism of contaminated particles induced damage of fused silica optics under high-power lasers, centers on the analysis of laser-particle interaction mechanism by morphology characterizations and COMSOL simulations, and improvement of laser resistance of optical elements.

Project 1

Research on the Mechanism of Al2O3 or Fe Particles Induced Damage of Fused Silica Optical Elements.

Project Details

Particles attached on the optical surface can seriously degrade the performance of optics under the high-power laser. In this project, Different microscopes are used to characterize their damage morphologies. The light distribution and thermal evolution of two different particles are simulated, focusing on better understanding their unique damage morphologies and underlying mechanisms.

Project 2

Research on the Mechanism of Silica Particles Induced Damage of Fused Silica Optical Elements.

Project Details

Particles on the front and back optical surfaces tend to induce different damage morphologies. This study showed that silica particles on the front surface tend to splash severely under high laser energy, while the particles on the back surface form large smooth pits on the substrate. Different damage mechanisms on the front and back optical surface were proposed.
